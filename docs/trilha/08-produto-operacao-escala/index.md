# Module 8 — Product, Operations and Scale

## What this module is about

You have spent seven modules learning to design intelligent systems. You can model domains, architect agents, build workflows, engineer context, evaluate quality, enforce safety, and govern responsibly. You know how to build the right thing the right way.

Now comes the question that separates a prototype from a product: **how do you launch it, keep it running, and grow it?**

Building an AI system is like constructing a hospital. The architecture, equipment, and protocols are essential — but a hospital only serves its purpose when the doors open and patients arrive. From that moment, everything changes. Real patients have real problems at unpredictable times. Equipment breaks. Staff get tired. New diseases appear. Regulations change. The community grows and needs more capacity.

This module teaches you to cross that bridge. From design to delivery. From blueprints to operations. From a single deployment to a system that scales across teams, domains, and markets.

This is the final module of the SSA core track. It brings together everything you have built and asks: can you turn your architecture into a product that delivers value, an operation that stays reliable, and a system that grows without breaking?

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

1. **Translate SSA architecture into product strategy** — aligning semantic design decisions with business goals, user needs, and market timing through structured roadmaps.
2. **Operate AI systems with discipline** — defining SLOs, ownership models, incident response protocols, and continuous improvement cycles that keep the system reliable in production.
3. **Scale with quality, risk, and cost control** — planning horizontal growth, multi-domain deployment, ontology evolution, and version management without losing the semantic integrity you worked so hard to build.

## What you will build

By the end of this module, you will have produced:

- A **two-quarter product roadmap** aligning architecture capabilities with business outcomes and user needs
- An **operational scorecard** with SLOs, alert definitions, ownership assignments, and escalation paths
- A **scale playbook** with growth scenarios, cost projections, and ontology evolution strategies
- A **launch plan** ready for stakeholder review
- An **operations plan** that could be handed to a team for production execution

## Prerequisites

**Module 7 — Security, Governance and Ethics** must be completed before starting this module. You need the governance framework, threat model, and control architecture from Module 7 because:

- Product launch decisions depend on your security and compliance posture
- Operational monitoring must include the guardrails and controls you designed
- Scaling introduces new risk surfaces that your governance model must address
- Incident response for AI systems connects directly to the incident runbook from Module 7

More broadly, this module synthesizes skills from all seven preceding modules. Your domain ontology (Module 2), agent architecture (Module 3), workflows (Module 4), context engineering (Module 5), evaluation framework (Module 6), and governance controls (Module 7) are the raw materials you will now package into a product, operate as a service, and prepare to scale.

10-12 hours (lessons + applications + assessment).
